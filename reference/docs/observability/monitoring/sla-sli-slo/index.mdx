# SLA · SLI · SLO

Internal targets and external commitments for everything the Billpay platform
exposes. Numbers are stated at the percentiles we measure to:

<span className="bp-pill">P50</span>

## What these terms mean

<div className="bp-defs">
  <div className="bp-def">
    <h4><code>SLA</code> — Service Level Agreement</h4>
    <p>The <strong>external commitment</strong> to consumers. If breached, it's incident-grade and contractually visible.</p>
  </div>
  <div className="bp-def">
    <h4><code>SLO</code> — Service Level Objective</h4>
    <p>The <strong>internal target</strong> engineering steers to. Tighter than the SLA by design — gives us headroom before customers notice.</p>
  </div>
  <div className="bp-def">
    <h4><code>SLI</code> — Service Level Indicator</h4>
    <p>The <strong>signal we actually measure</strong> — the metric that proves whether we're meeting the SLO.</p>
  </div>
</div>

:::info[Quick reference]
- **SLA** is the promise. **SLO** is the goal. **SLI** is the measurement.
- Latency cells in the sub-pages are **SLO targets** — internal numbers.
- The availability pill on each card is the **SLA** — the external commitment.
- The "SLI" line under each card describes what we measure to produce these numbers.
- Numbers are **indicative targets** — confirm the latest contracted values with the Billpay team before quoting externally.
:::

## How to read a card

Each entry has:

1. **Title** — the function or API name (the endpoint sits underneath as a smaller note).
2. **SLA availability pill** — the contractual uptime.
3. **A 5-cell latency grid** — SLO targets at P50, P90, P99, P99.9, P99.99.
4. **The SLI** — the metric + the error budget.

## Error budget at a glance

| SLA target | Allowable downtime / 30 days | Allowable downtime / year |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **99.99%** | ~4.3 minutes | ~52 minutes |
| **99.95%** | ~21.6 minutes | ~4.4 hours |
| **99.9%**  | ~43.2 minutes | ~8.8 hours |

:::tip[Operationally]
The **SLO** is what engineering steers to. The **SLA** is what the platform
commits to externally. We deliberately leave headroom — if our SLO is being
breached but our SLA isn't, that's the cue to fix things **before** customers
notice.
:::
